# 💙 Trauma Blog & Resource Site — Outline & Plan

**Project Name Ideas (pick one or suggest your own):**
- *Rising Through It* — A Trauma Survivor's Blog & Resource Hub
- *After the Storm* — Stories, Resources & Hope for Trauma Survivors
- *Still Standing* — A Community for Those Who've Been Through the Unthinkable
- *The Other Side* — A Blog for Survivors of Loss, Trauma & Hardship

---

## 🎯 Purpose & Mission

A free, honest, deeply personal blog and resource platform for people who have experienced:
- **Childhood trauma** (abuse, neglect, adverse childhood experiences)
- **Loss of a child** (overdose, illness, accident — grief with no roadmap)
- **Financial devastation** (fraud, Ponzi schemes, foreclosure, starting over)
- **Compounding hardship** — when one tragedy leads to another

The site will not preach. It will not be clinical. It will be **real** — written by someone who has lived it.

---

## 👥 Target Audience

| Who They Are | What They Need |
|---|---|
| Parents who lost a child | To feel seen, not alone; grief resources |
| Adult survivors of childhood abuse | Validation, stories, healing pathways |
| Families hit by financial fraud/loss | Practical resources, shared experience |
| Caregivers and spouses of trauma survivors | How to support without burning out |
| Anyone rebuilding after devastating loss | Hope, community, practical next steps |

## 🗂️ Site Structure (Pages)

### 1. 🏠 Home Page
- Short, powerful opening statement (your story in 2–3 sentences)
- "You are not alone" message
- Featured recent posts
- Call-to-action: Subscribe / Share Your Story

### 2. 📖 Tommy & Gina's Story *(About Page)*
- Who you are, what you've been through — in your own words
- Why you built this site
- Photo (optional — add when comfortable)
- Note about your son

### 3. 📝 Blog
Categories of posts (see content plan below)

### 4. 💡 Resources
Curated, vetted links to:
- **Grief support** (The Compassionate Friends, GriefShare, SAMHSA)
- **Substance use & overdose loss** (GRASP, Alliance of Hope)
- **Childhood trauma** (RAINN, ACEs Connection, local therapy finders)
- **Financial fraud recovery** (AARP Fraud Watch Network, FTC resources)
- **Mental health** (NAMI, Crisis Text Line: text HOME to 741741, 988 Lifeline)

### 5. 🤝 Community / Share Your Story
- Simple form for visitors to submit their own story (optional, reviewed before publishing)
- Guest post from other survivors
- Comment section on blog posts

### 6. 📬 Contact / Connect
- Email form
- Social media links (when ready)
- Note welcoming collaboration

## 🛠️ Technology Options (Free / Low Cost)

| Platform | Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **WordPress.com** (Free tier) | $0 | Quickest start, no coding needed |
| **Ghost.org** (Starter) | $9/mo | Clean, modern blog feel |
| **GitHub Pages + Jekyll** | $0 | Full control, uses your existing GitHub skills |
| **Hashnode** | $0 | Free developer-focused blogging |
| **Substack** | $0 (free tier) | Blog + newsletter combo, great for building audience |

**Recommendation for you:** Start with **GitHub Pages + a simple HTML/CSS template** (you have the web dev skills), or **WordPress.com free** to get the content live immediately while you build the custom version.

## 📣 How to Build an Audience (Free Methods)

1. **Share on Facebook** — Grief groups, trauma survivor groups, parenting groups
2. **Reddit communities** — r/GriefSupport, r/CPTSD, r/ParentingAfterLoss
3. **Join The Compassionate Friends** and mention the blog
4. **Connect with other trauma bloggers** — guest post swaps
5. **SEO basics** — use the exact phrases people Google when they're hurting
